## PART 1: CHARACTER OVERVIEW & NARRATIVE ROLE

### Who JP Morgan Is

**Real-World Context:**
JPMorgan Chase is the largest bank in the United States with \\$4+ trillion in assets. CEO Jamie Dimon has publicly stated Bitcoin is a \\\"fraud.\\\" The bank represents the ultimate traditional finance power structure—sophisticated, wealthy, and fundamentally opposed to decentralized currency systems.

**Narrative Meaning:**
JP Morgan isn't just skeptical—they're **strategically opposed** to Bitcoin. Unlike S&P (mechanically incompetent) or Peter Schiff (individually deluded), JP Morgan represents **intelligent opposition from entrenched power**. They *understand* Bitcoin but refuse it because Bitcoin threatens their business model.

**Story Arc Role:**
- **Panels 14-16:** Visible in background as shadow threat (emerging opposition)
- **Panel 17-18:** Direct confrontation with Saylor (sophisticated strategic challenge)
- **Panel 19:** Final battle during shareholder spirit bomb (institutional power vs. collective belief)
- **Panel 20-21:** Defeated or reformed (power structures must adapt to new paradigm)

**What JP Morgan Represents:**
\\\"The sophisticated enemy. They know exactly what they're fighting. They have resources, intelligence, and institutional weight. But they're defending an obsolete model against infinite duration.\\\"

### Core Philosophy: Intelligent Resistance

JP Morgan is **different from other enemies** because they're **not mechanically blind like S&P** and **not individually deluded like Peter Schiff**. They're **strategically correct within their own paradigm** but their paradigm is finite.

**Visual Manifestation:**
- Elegant armor (sophisticated strategy)
- Intelligent facial features (they understand the threat)
- Dangerous composure (controlled power)
- No mechanical rigidity (can adapt tactics, but won't change fundamental opposition)
- Solitary nature (represents concentrated banking power)

- Therefore: **Defeat via asymmetric warfare**

- Won 2008 crisis

- Assumes modern threats operate under old rules
- Bitcoin breaks all old rules
- Therefore: **Defeat via rule-breaking innovation**

**Critical Weakness #4: Requires Conventional Resources**
- Every action costs capital from reserves
- Shareholder collective grows without consuming resources
- Eventually institutional resources exhausted
- Shareholder belief never diminishes
- Therefore: **Defeat via resource outlasting**
